### 📐 Architectural Principles & Engineering Philosophy

My approach to building software revolves around scalability, maintainability, and clear separation of concerns:

*   **Clean & Hexagonal Architecture:** Keeping core domain logic framework-free and isolated from external databases, UI libraries, or transport protocols. This ensures business rules remain testable, predictable, and resilient to technology changes.
*   **Domain-Driven Design (DDD):** Modeling complex business domains through bounded contexts, rich aggregates, and ubiquitous language. Aligning software structure with the actual business logic to reduce cognitive load and translation overhead.
*   **Event-Driven & Microservices:** Designing decoupled distributed systems that communicate asynchronously. Using events to establish eventual consistency, handle scale gracefully, and minimize system-wide coupling.
*   **Quality & Test-Driven Development (TDD):** Writing clean, self-documenting code supported by a robust testing strategy (from fast in-process unit/integration tests to targeted E2E tests).
*   **Empathetic Mentorship & Leadership:** Leading engineering teams by establishing alignment, encouraging ownership, and fostering continuous learning through constructive peer reviews and shared architectural designs.
